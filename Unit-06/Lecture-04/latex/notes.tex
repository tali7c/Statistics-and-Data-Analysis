ARIMA models and forecasting workflow (overview).

  \item Define ARIMA(p,d,q) at a high level
  \item Explain differencing (d) to remove trend

\section*{Detailed Notes}
These notes are designed to be read alongside the slides. They expand each slide bullet into
plain-language explanations, small worked examples, and common pitfalls. When a formula
appears, emphasize (1) what each symbol means, (2) the assumptions needed to use it, and (3)
how to interpret the final number in the problem context.

\section*{ARIMA}
\begin{itemize}
  \item p: AR order
  \item d: differencing order
  \item q: MA order
\end{itemize}

\section*{Differencing}
\begin{itemize}
  \item First difference: y\_t - y\_{t-1}
  \item Often stabilizes mean
  \item Over-differencing adds noise
\end{itemize}
